Syllabus

The SAT is a standardized test widely used for college admissions in the United States. The test covers four main areas: Reading, Writing and Language, Math, and an optional Essay. Here is an overview of the content areas covered in each section of the SAT:

Reading:

The Reading Test consists of five passages in the following categories: U.S. and World Literature, History and Social Studies, and Science. The passages are accompanied by multiple-choice questions that test your understanding of the passage as well as your vocabulary.

Writing and Language:

The Writing and Language Test consists of four passages that focus on a variety of topics, including career, social issues, and education. The passages are accompanied by multiple-choice questions that test your ability to revise and edit written text for grammar, punctuation, and word choice.

Math:

The Math Test consists of two main sections: a no-calculator section and a calculator-allowed section. The no-calculator section tests your understanding of math concepts and your ability to solve problems without the use of a calculator. The calculator-allowed section tests your ability to use a calculator to solve more complex math problems. The Math Test covers a range of math topics, including algebra, geometry, and data analysis.

Essay (optional):

The Essay is a writing task that measures your ability to write a well-organized, coherent, and developed essay.
